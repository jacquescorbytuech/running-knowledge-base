# Stephen Seiler

> [!info] Sport scientist
> Described the polarised, roughly 80/20 distribution of elite endurance athletes. He measured what elites do; the leap to "everyone should train 80/20" is a separate, contested claim.

Stephen Seiler is an American-Norwegian sport scientist whose research described, rather than invented, the training-intensity distribution of endurance athletes. The often-cited early study was small and specific: 11 nationally competitive junior cross-country skiers, not a broad elite cohort ([Seiler & Kjerland 2006](../sources/seiler-2006-polarised.md)). The roughly 80% easy, 20% hard pattern was generalised across his own and others' later observational work into the empirical basis for the [polarised and 80/20](../techniques/polarised-training.md) discussion ([Seiler 2010](../sources/seiler-2010-distribution.md)).

The distinction between his descriptive science and the prescriptive popularisations built on it matters. Seiler measured what works for elites; the leap to "therefore everyone should train exactly 80/20" is a separate, more contested claim. He has been a participant in the open debate over whether polarised training is genuinely optimal, which remains unresolved. See [training philosophies](../techniques/training-philosophies.md).
