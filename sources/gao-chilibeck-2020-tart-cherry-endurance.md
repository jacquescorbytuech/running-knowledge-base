# Gao & Chilibeck 2020, Tart cherry concentrate and endurance exercise performance meta-analysis

A meta-analysis of 10 studies (127 men, 20 women; aged 18–35, mostly endurance-trained cyclists, runners and triathletes) on Montmorency tart cherry concentrate and endurance performance, published in the *Journal of the American College of Nutrition*. Tart cherry taken as juice or powder, over about seven days up to 1.5 hours before exercise, produced a small but significant improvement in endurance performance. The authors attribute the effect to anthocyanins' antioxidant and anti-inflammatory action and to improved blood flow via nitric oxide.

## Key takeaways

- Pooled across 10 trials, tart cherry concentrate gave a small significant improvement in endurance exercise performance.
- Participants were mostly young trained endurance athletes; the female sample was small (20 of 147).
- Proposed mechanisms are anthocyanin antioxidant/anti-inflammatory effects and enhanced nitric-oxide-mediated blood flow and muscle oxygenation.
- The same antioxidant action that may aid recovery is the basis for the concern that high-dose polyphenol supplements can blunt training adaptation.
- Much of the public messaging around this literature is industry-promoted; the primary trials are small.
