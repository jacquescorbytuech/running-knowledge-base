# Frontiers Media, publishing model and reputation (overview)

An overview of the Swiss open-access publisher Frontiers Media, founded in 2007, covering the article-processing-charge model, the special-issues and rapid-acceptance practices that have drawn peer-review criticism, and the institutional responses to it. Used as a citable anchor for the "grey-zone publisher" phenomenon that also implicates other high-volume open-access publishers.

## Key takeaways

- Frontiers runs an author-pays open-access model and had over 220 journals by 2023.
- It was added to Beall's list of possible predatory publishers in October 2015; the list itself was shut down in January 2017, reportedly under pressure that included Frontiers.
- Rapid acceptance in special issues has repeatedly raised peer-review-quality concerns.
- In January 2023 Inria labelled Frontiers a "grey-zone publisher" urging vigilance, and Zhejiang Gongshang University excluded Frontiers (alongside MDPI and Hindawi) articles from researcher evaluation.
- High-profile failures include a 2024 retraction of an article carrying AI-generated figures.
