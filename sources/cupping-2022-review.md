# Cupping therapy in musculoskeletal and sports rehabilitation 2022 (systematic review)

Systematic, evidence-based review of cupping therapy in musculoskeletal and sports rehabilitation. Most included trials were of low methodological quality and high risk of bias. Cupping outperformed passive comparators such as no treatment or usual care, but its effect was similar to that of acupuncture, and the evidence was insufficient to recommend it for athletes. Adverse events (skin marking, haematoma, occasional increased pain) were generally minor. Weak, low-quality evidence consistent with a largely non-specific effect.
