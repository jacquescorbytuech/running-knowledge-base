# Hunter & Smith 2007, self-optimised stride frequency

Runners self-select a stride frequency at or close to the one that minimises their oxygen cost, and the freely chosen frequency tracks the individual metabolic optimum during prolonged running. Confirms the long-standing Cavanagh & Williams finding that economical cadence is individual and largely self-tuned. A controlled metabolic study.
