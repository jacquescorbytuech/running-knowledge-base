# Daniels 1984, stride-rate observations at the Olympics

Jack Daniels counted stride rates of distance runners at the 1984 Olympics and found nearly all of about 47 elite athletes ran at or above 180 steps per minute at race pace. The widely cited origin of the 180 figure. An observational count of elites at racing speed, not a controlled study and not a recommendation for recreational paces.
