# Stults-Kolehmainen & Bartholomew 2012, Stress and muscular recovery

Experimental study in resistance-trained people. Those reporting higher chronic life stress recovered muscular function more slowly after a hard bout, but stress did not significantly affect perceived fatigue or soreness — only the physical measure of maximal isometric force was impaired. Demonstrates that non-training stress moderates physical recovery. Small sample, but a controlled design. Moderate.
