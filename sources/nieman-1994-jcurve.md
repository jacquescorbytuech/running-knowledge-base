# Nieman 1994, Exercise, infection and the J-curve

Proposes the "J-curve": moderate training lowers upper-respiratory-infection risk below sedentary levels, while heavy or prolonged exertion raises it above average. Influential, but the upturn at high loads rests largely on observational, symptom-based data, and the model is now contested. Contested.
