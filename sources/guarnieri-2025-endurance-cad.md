# Guarnieri et al. 2025, Extreme endurance training and coronary artery disease: systematic review and meta-analysis

This PRISMA-guided systematic review and meta-analysis (MEDLINE, Embase, Scopus) found that endurance athletes show a higher prevalence of coronary atherosclerosis than non-athlete controls, but do not show a greater prevalence of high-risk or obstructive plaques. The authors conclude that elevated coronary calcium scores in athletes reflect stable, densely calcified plaque rather than the vulnerable, soft plaques that precipitate acute cardiac events. Evidence: moderate.
