# Pérez-Gómez et al. 2020, Ashwagandha and VO₂max systematic review and meta-analysis

A systematic review (5 studies, 162 participants) and meta-analysis (4 studies, 142 participants) of *Withania somnifera* (ashwagandha) supplementation and maximal oxygen uptake, published in *Nutrients*. Pooled VO₂max improved by a mean difference of 3.00 mL·kg⁻¹·min⁻¹ (95% CI 0.18–5.82, p = 0.04), but with very high heterogeneity (I² = 84%) and evidence graded low by GRADE. Every included trial was conducted in India.

## Key takeaways

- Ashwagandha significantly increased VO₂max versus placebo (mean difference 3.00 mL·kg⁻¹·min⁻¹, 95% CI 0.18–5.82, p = 0.04) but the confidence interval nearly touches zero and heterogeneity was very high (I² = 84%).
- Doses ranged 300–1000 mg/day over 2–12 weeks; extracts included KSM-66, aqueous root extract, and root powder.
- All trials were run in India, in healthy adults and athletes (cyclists, hockey players), aged 16–45.
- Authors rated overall evidence quality low (GRADE), citing few studies, small samples, high heterogeneity and unclear blinding.
- Conclusion is cautious: ashwagandha "might improve" VO₂max, pending better trials.
