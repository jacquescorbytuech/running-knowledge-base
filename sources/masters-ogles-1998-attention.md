# Masters & Ogles 1998, Associative and dissociative strategies in running

Review of attentional strategies in running. Associative attention (monitoring bodily signals such as breathing and pace) tends to support faster racing, while dissociation (distraction) lowers perceived exertion and can make easy running more tolerable; attention shifts naturally toward association as intensity rises. Results are mixed and context-dependent, with no universal winner. Limited.
