# Edwards et al. 2018, Social facilitation of endurance and sprint exercise

Laboratory study (n=12) of self-paced cycling exercise. Active verbal encouragement raised average power output and increased motivation and perceived exertion compared with exercising without it. A small sample, but the effect direction is consistent with the long-established social-facilitation finding that the presence of and encouragement from others lifts effort on well-practised tasks. Moderate.
