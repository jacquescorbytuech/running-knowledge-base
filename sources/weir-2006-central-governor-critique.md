# Weir et al. 2006, critique of the central governor model

Critical review of the central governor model. Argues the model oversimplifies by routing all fatigue through one central mechanism, and favours task dependency: central and peripheral factors both contribute, their balance shifting with the type, intensity and duration of the task. A review and conceptual critique.
