# Humans optimise ground contact time and leg stiffness to minimise the metabolic cost of running

Curvilinear relationships link ground contact time and leg stiffness to metabolic cost; most trained runners self-select values within about 5% of their mathematical optimum, suggesting gait is already largely self-optimised for economy. Tempers the idea that deliberate form changes will easily improve economy. Moderate.
