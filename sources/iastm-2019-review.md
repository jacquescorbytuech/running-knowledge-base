# Instrument-assisted soft-tissue mobilisation 2019 (systematic review and effect-size analysis)

Systematic review and effect-size analysis of instrument-assisted soft-tissue mobilisation (IASTM), the family of techniques that includes the Graston Technique. The studies were few, heterogeneous and used many different instruments and protocols, so no firm clinical recommendation on dosage or technique could be made, and IASTM showed little consistent advantage over comparison treatments for pain or function. The evidence is too thin and inconsistent to validate the method itself, as distinct from the exercise it is usually delivered alongside.
