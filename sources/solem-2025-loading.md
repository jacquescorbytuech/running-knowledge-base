# Solem et al. 2025, Carbohydrate loading meta-analysis

Loading raises muscle glycogen substantially: cycling increased stores by ~270 mmol/kg dry weight and running by ~157 mmol/kg dry weight across 30 studies; running supercompensates less than cycling; more than 8 g/kg/day may suffice. Moderate-strong.
