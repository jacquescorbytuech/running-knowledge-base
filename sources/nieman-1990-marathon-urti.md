# Nieman et al. 1990, Infectious episodes around the Los Angeles Marathon

Prospective field study around the Los Angeles Marathon. In the week after the race, finishers reported far more upper-respiratory-tract-infection symptoms than similarly trained runners who did not race (about 13% versus 2%, odds ratio near 6). Based on self-reported symptoms rather than laboratory-confirmed infection. Moderate.
