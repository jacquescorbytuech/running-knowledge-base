# De Sousa et al. 2014, Moisture-wicking fabric in the heat

Acute exercise study comparing a synthetic moisture-wicking shirt with cotton during moderate exercise in the heat. The wicking fabric retained less sweat and modestly improved thermoregulation, supporting synthetics for comfort and chafe reduction rather than for performance. A small, single-intensity study measuring physiology, not race outcomes. Limited.
