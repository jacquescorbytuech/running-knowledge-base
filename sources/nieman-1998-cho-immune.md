# Nieman 1998, influence of carbohydrate on the immune response to prolonged exercise

Carbohydrate ingestion during intensive, prolonged exercise sustained plasma glucose and attenuated the cortisol and growth-hormone response, with fewer perturbations in blood immune-cell counts and inflammatory activity, versus placebo. Author noted clinical significance awaited further research. Exercise Immunology Review. Moderate.
