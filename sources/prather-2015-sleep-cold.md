# Prather et al. 2015, Sleep and susceptibility to the common cold

Experimental-challenge study. People sleeping under six hours a night were markedly more likely to develop a cold after controlled viral exposure than those sleeping more than seven hours. Direct evidence that short sleep raises infection susceptibility, supporting sleep as one of the better-evidenced levers for staying well. Moderate.
